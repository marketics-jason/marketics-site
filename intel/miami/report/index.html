Market Context

What the data actually means

Miami's 62% occupancy holds flat while supply grows by 25% year-over-year. That's not a coincidence — it means demand is absorbing nearly 2,300 new listings as fast as they enter the market. The environment is structurally healthy, but it's getting more competitive. The gap between an optimized listing and a passive one is widening every quarter.

The booking window tells a critical strategy story. At 18 days — short by most vacation market standards — Miami guests make fast decisions. That compressed timeline rewards listings that convert on first impression: strong photography, an immediately clear value proposition, and pricing that feels right on a quick scan. Properties that require a guest to do the math lose the comparison.

The dynamic pricing data is the single most revealing signal in this report: 44% of listings use high dynamic pricing and capture 62% of all bookings. That 18-point gap isn't coincidence — it's the measurable return on optimization. The 21% of listings with no pricing strategy compete for just 10% of the booking pool. In a market where ADR spans from $139 to $295+ across seasons, flat pricing is a structural disadvantage on every search.

And then there's June 2026. FIFA World Cup matches bring a once-in-a-generation demand event to Miami. The pricing decisions you make in the next 30 days directly determine your FIFA revenue. The booking window for international travellers is significantly longer than Miami's 18-day median — they're comparing listings right now.

Dynamic pricing is the highest-leverage variable Miami's ADR spans from $139 to $295+ across seasons. Flat pricing means systematically overpricing in slow periods and undercharging during peak demand — a losing position in both directions.
02
Fee structure affects search visibility and conversion Split-fee listings represent 59% of supply but capture 67% of bookings. Host-only fee structures hold 41% of supply but only 33% of bookings. In a last-minute, price-sensitive market, how total cost appears in search results materially changes whether a guest clicks through or scrolls past.
03
Larger properties require an entirely different strategy 4BR and 5BR properties book 31–43 days out vs. 16 days for studios and 1BRs. That longer booking horizon demands different minimum-stay logic, different discount ladder timing, and different cancellation policy positioning. Running a 4BR on the same settings as a 1BR is the most common pricing mistake in this segment.
04
Self check-in is the most desired missing amenity 11.9% of Miami guests actively seek self check-in and frequently can't find it. In an 18-day booking window market, frictionless arrival is a conversion differentiator — not a nice-to-have. Smart lock installation typically recovers its cost within 2–3 additional bookings.
