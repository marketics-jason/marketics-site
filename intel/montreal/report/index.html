Market Analysis

What the Data
Actually Tells You

The headline metrics look like a softening market — ADR down $13, RevPAR down $10, occupancy down 2 points year-over-year. The correct interpretation is more precise: supply grew faster than demand. Active listings are up 1,503 (+36%) while bookings are up 38,444 (+33%). More competition, same guest pool, marginal pressure on rates and occupancy. That's a positioning story, not a demand story. The total demand is robust.

The booking window data tells a more specific story for event strategy. The median booking window is 24 days — up 3 days year-over-year, and accelerating for peak summer dates. For F1 specifically, market behavior shows that the highest-rate bookings arrive significantly ahead of the 24-day median. Hosts who price race weekend correctly in April are competing for a different guest pool than hosts who update their calendars in May. Both pools exist. They do not overlap.

The dynamic pricing gap in this market is the clearest data point in the entire dataset. 35% of Montréal Airbnb listings operate with no dynamic pricing whatsoever. Those listings capture 13% of total market bookings. Listings with high dynamic pricing — just 34% of the market — capture 61% of bookings. This is not a marginal advantage. It is the primary structural differentiator in a market where location and property quality alone no longer guarantee a booking.

The F1 Grand Prix is the accelerant. May 22–24, 2026. 370,000 fans. Every hotel within walking distance of Circuit Gilles Villeneuve at capacity by late April. Guests who want an Airbnb — which is where the real money is — are comparing listings right now. The comparison happens once. The booking happens once. The host who wins that comparison is the host whose pricing, photos, listing copy, and amenity profile are optimized before the guest opens a tab.

01
Supply Grew, Not Demand
ADR is down, but bookings are up 38,444. The market isn't cooling — it's getting more competitive. The hosts gaining share are the ones with dynamic pricing and optimized listings. The hosts losing share are the ones who haven't changed their setup in two years.
02
3BR+ Hosts Are Overpricing
The data shows 3-bedroom and 4-bedroom listings where the median listed price exceeds the median booked nightly price — meaning hosts are losing bookings by pricing above market clearing rates. This is a comp-set calibration issue, not a property quality issue.
03
Weekends Are Not Equal
Friday occupancy runs 67.3% and Saturday 69.4% versus 55% mid-week. The price factor on Fridays is 176.5 versus 131–134 on Tuesdays and Wednesdays. A flat weekly rate leaves significant Friday–Saturday premiums uncaptured.
04
Long Stays Drive Volume
29+ day stays represent the single largest booking bucket by nights booked (303,100 nights). The 1-day booking cohort is the smallest. Minimum stay rules set too short push out longer, higher-value guests in favour of transient single-night bookings.
