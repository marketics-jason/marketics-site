Act 1 — The Situation

A boutique hotel at 43% occupancy in a city that has strong STR demand. The math doesn't work at that occupancy. Closure was not a hypothetical.

The property had everything — character rooms, unique wallpaper, architectural detail, a Plateau address. What it didn't have was a language the platform understood. Hotels on Airbnb compete against purpose-built short-term rentals. Most hotels list themselves the way they'd list on Booking.com — room type, amenity list, price.

That is the wrong language for Airbnb's search environment.

Airbnb guests are not booking a room. They are booking an experience, a neighborhood, a story. The hotel was speaking the wrong language to the platform that controlled its survival.

Act 2 — The Repositioning
01
Visual Identity Per Unit
The photography — unique wallpaper, character rooms, architectural detail — became the hook, not an afterthought. Each room was listed with its own visual identity. The heron room. The velvet room. The terrace suite. Guests don't book "Room 102." They book a story.
02
Platform Language
The listing was repositioned from "hotel room in Montréal" to "design-forward suites in the Plateau." Every description was rewritten in Airbnb's native language — experience-first, neighborhood-specific, design-led. The same rooms. Completely different perception.
03
Pricing Strategy
Hotel pricing defaults to rack rates and seasonal adjustments. STR pricing is dynamic — it responds to search volume, competitor occupancy, event calendars, and booking windows. The hotel's pricing was rebuilt from scratch on STR logic.
